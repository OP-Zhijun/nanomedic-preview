Overview

Research profile

Prof. Yang's laboratory develops high-performance hybrid drug delivery systems, space-medicine platforms, and stimuli-responsive smart medical devices. The group specializes in drug synthesis, medical device design, and biomaterial preparation — translating polymer nanomedicine into clinical and space-health applications. Current focus areas include cancer immunotherapy, organelle-targeted therapeutics, and spaceflight-accelerated aging research.

Education

  • 1989–1993: B.S. in Pharmacy, Chungang University College of Pharmacy, Korea
  • 1993–1995: M.S. in Pharmaceutics, Chungang University College of Pharmacy, Korea
  • 2002–2006: Ph.D. in Pharmaceutics, Seoul National University College of Pharmacy, Korea
  • 2006–2009: Post-Doctoral Researcher, Southwestern Medical Center at Dallas, TX

Professional Experience

  • 1995–2001: Senior Researcher, Pharmaceutical Engineering Lab., CKD Research Institute, Chong Kun Dang Pharm., Korea
  • 2001–2002: Chief Researcher, Dept. of Pharmaceutics and Drug Delivery, R&D center, CJ 제일, Korea
  • 2009–2012: R&D Center Vice Director, Utah and Inha DDS and Therapeutics, Korea
  • 2012–present: Professor, Inha University College of Medicine

Research Interests

  • Protein / peptide drug delivery systems
  • Photo-responsive polymeric drug delivery
  • Nanomedicine and nanoparticle formulation
  • Drug-eluting medical devices
  • Bio-adhesive hydrogels
  • Immune target therapy · polymer–drug conjugate · photo-responsive therapy (self-declared, Google Scholar)
